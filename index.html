Partner Evaluation Checklist

Critical questions before signing contracts

✅ WHAT TO ASK BEFORE SIGNING

🔧 TECHNICAL

  • API uptime SLA documented (>99.9%)?
  • Sandbox environment for testing?
  • Your tech stack supported?
  • Scalability limits defined?
  • Integration timeline realistic?
  • Documentation quality verified?
  • Load testing and performance benchmarks?

🔒 SECURITY & COMPLIANCE

  • Penetration testing completed? (Request latest report)
  • Security certifications? (ISO 27001, SOC 2, PCI-DSS)
  • Vulnerability management process documented?
  • Data encryption at rest and in transit?
  • Access control and authentication protocols?
  • Incident response plan and SLAs?
  • Third-party security audits conducted?
  • Bug bounty program active?

💰 COMMERCIAL

  • Revenue share vs flat fee model clear?
  • Minimum volume commitment?
  • Pricing tiers documented?
  • Contract length and exit clauses?
  • Performance penalties specified?
  • Hidden fees disclosed?

⚖️ REGULATORY (MAS)

  • MAS licensed? (Request license number)
  • Your liability coverage defined?
  • Data residency in Singapore?
  • Audit rights in contract?
  • Compliance reporting automated?
  • AML/KYC procedures documented?

🛠️ OPERATIONAL

  • Dedicated support team assigned?
  • Onboarding timeline with penalties?
  • Case studies from similar companies?
  • SLAs for issue resolution?
  • Disaster recovery plan documented?
  • Migration support if switching?

Implementation Roadmap

Your next steps toward launching embedded finance

🎭 Your Role in the Ecosystem

Understanding which role you want to play helps determine your build vs partner strategy:

🏦 Incumbents/Banks (Foundation)

Role: Licensed entities, capital-rich, own settlement infrastructure (e.g., FAST, PayNow)

Examples: DBS, UOB, OCBC, Licensed Digital Banks

🔧 BaaS/PaaS Platforms (Enablers)

Role: Provide technology, compliance and regulatory bridges (APIs) to non-financial entities

Examples: Mambu, Thought Machine, HolyWally, Aspire, Nium

💡 Fintech Specialists (Innovations)

Role: Focus on specific financial verticals (e.g., P2P Lending, BNPL, Remittance)

Examples: Funding Societies, Atome, Wise, Endowus

🌐 Non-Financial Platforms (Distribution & Data)

Role: Own the customer relationship, data and distribution interface

Examples: Grab, Shopee, Telcos, Large E-commerce/Retailers

🛡️ RegTech/Infrastructure (Support)

Role: KYC, compliance, credit scoring, fraud detection, cybersecurity

Examples: CredoLab, Sumsub, Jumio, TrustingSocial, FinBots.ai

💡 Key Insight: Most organizations succeed by playing to their strengths. If you own customer distribution (like Grab/Shopee), partner for infrastructure. If you have banking licenses, enable others through BaaS. Choose your battles wisely!

💰 Investment Considerations

Setup Costs

  • Platform integration fees
  • Professional services (Rule: $1 software = $3 services)
  • Compliance/licensing
  • Custom development (if needed)

Ongoing Costs

  • Monthly platform fees
  • Transaction fees (% of GMV)
  • Operating costs (support, ops)
  • Regulatory compliance

💡 Build vs Partner Economics

Partner Approach: Lower upfront ($20K-$100K setup), higher per-transaction costs (2-4% of GMV)
Build Approach: Higher upfront ($200K-$1M+), lower marginal costs long-term

Break-even: Typically at 10,000-50,000 transactions/month. Consider your volume projections carefully!

📋 Immediate Actions (Week 1-2)

1. Share with Your Team
Present this stack to your CTO, product team, and compliance officer. Use the checklist for partner evaluation discussions.
2. Test Provider Sandboxes
Request sandbox access from 2-3 shortlisted providers. Test basic integrations to validate technical feasibility.
3. Map Customer Journey
Identify exactly where in your customer experience the embedded service adds value. Design the integration points.
4. Run Financial Modeling
Calculate: Expected revenue, Customer LTV increase, Implementation costs, Break-even timeline
